Automated Lab Data Processor

Bench to Bytes portfolio analysis report. Uses only public or synthetic data.

Hypothesis

A plate-reader workflow can flag assay quality and prepare dose-response data without manual spreadsheet handling.

Data

Synthetic 96-well plate-reader data generated from standard public assay design patterns: vehicle controls, positive controls, two compounds, eight doses, and triplicates.

Process

  1. Generated tidy well-level absorbance data.
  2. Calculated Z-factor from positive and negative controls.
  3. Calculated replicate CV percent by compound and dose.
  4. Saved QC summaries and a labelled figure for rapid assay review.

Outputs

  • data/plate_reader.csv
  • outputs/qc_summary.csv
  • outputs/replicate_cv.csv
  • figures/qc_summary.svg

Interpretation

The simulated plate has a strong Z-factor and moderate replicate variability, indicating the synthetic assay is suitable for demonstrating automated QC and downstream curve fitting.
